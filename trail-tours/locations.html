Locations

The Idaho wilderness provides a wide variety of terrain. From rugged, rocky hills to vast, flat meadows and anything in between, there’s something for everyone. Experienced riders can take on challenging trails and those who are just starting out can cruise on something a little easier. Idaho’s trails can lead you to beautiful places. Hidden mountain lakes, sharp peaks, and peaceful wildflower meadows are all sights just waiting for you to behold. If you’re wondering where to ride, we’ve got you covered.

Spencer, Idaho

Located just an hour north of Idaho Falls, Spencer, Idaho features a range of motorized trails and mountain roads. Founded in 1879, Spencer was a railroad town. Large deposits of opals and precious gems were discovered in the area in 1948, and Spencer famously contains some of the largest opal mines in the United States. Along with good trail rides and camping, tourists can have the unique experience of mining for their own opals at the Spencer Opal Mine as well.

Much of Spencer’s trails can be found in the Stoddard Creek camping area. Stoddard Creek is located a few minutes past the initial town of Spencer. If you continue travelling on I-15, you’ll come across the Stoddard Creek exit. Stoddard Creek features a campground that is ran by the US Forest Service as well as suitable camping spots along the road. Among the many trails in this area, one of the most popular is the City of Rocks trail. This trail provides a lengthy ride up a rocky mountain. It’s on the more challenging side, but it leads to the top of a shale covered hill that overlooks the mountainous valley. The “City of Rocks” is a collection of pathways and buildings built out of shale by people who have ridden the trail.

The City of Rocks is just some of what Spencer has to offer. Our guides can take you and your group on an adventure through one of Idaho’s most beautiful areas!

Kelly Canyon, Idaho

Kelly Canyon is a lot more than just a ski and mountain biking resort. The Kelly Canyon area is full of motorized vehicle trails. Close by, it’s only about 30 minutes from Idaho Falls, near the small town of Ririe. Tourists can enjoy the Heise Hot Springs and campground. Pizza, zip-lining, and much more can be enjoyed after your ride through the canyon.

One of the most popular trails in the area is a 4-mile loop. It’s a fun, intermediate ride that takes about 2 hours to complete. The loop contains a range of different terrains. Much of it runs through the forested hills and alongside the Snake River.

One of the area’s more challenging rides is the Stinking Springs trail. It’s a 10-mile adventure and takes about 5 hours to complete. This trail is rather steep and includes some switchbacks that are difficult to traverse. It’s perfect for intermediate-advanced and advanced riders.

This canyon is full of trails that can satisfy any skill level. Its long loops and trails provide a whole day of fun. There’s something new to try each time you come!

Swan Valley, Idaho

Swan Valley is one of Idaho’s prettiest areas. It’s only about an hour east of Idaho Falls and provides a wide range of recreational activities. It’s a popular area for camping, boating, and of course, trail riding. The small town that sits near the south fork of the Snake River is a base camp for all those who enjoy the activities that the valley offers. Travelers have access to an RV park in town, gas, food, and of course, the well-known square ice cream at Rainey Creek.

Past the town, visitors can camp and ride near the Palisades reservoir. The area features many trails and dirt roads that riders can enjoy. The easier rides provide a beautiful scenic traverse through the woods. If you happen to go in late September or early October, the trees are painted in reds, yellows, and oranges, and are a captivating sight of bright, fall colors.

There’s plenty of challenging terrain for more experienced riders as well. The Fall Creek trail takes riders on a long and windy adventure. The trail is steep and covered with loose rock. It takes riders up into the mountains and along the rugged crests. This ride is no easy feat, but the views at the top are well worth it.
