Safety

Committed to Keeping You Safe

Equipment

  • Every rider is required to wear a well-fitting, DOT certified riding helmet.
  • Every rider is required to wear gloves.
  • Every rider is required to wear good, sturdy shoes that are free from any holes or tears. Those riding motorcycles are required to wear riding boots.
  • Long sleeve shirts are preferred, but not required.
  • If a rider wishes to bring a backpack, the pack must be small to medium sized and must have a chest buckle to secure it.

Experience

  • Trail tours welcomes unexperienced riders who want to try something new. Before they can begin, however, they must spend time with an instructor.
  • If a trail becomes too difficult for your experience level, be honest and notify your guide. If a guide feels that trail conditions are too poor, or notices a rider struggling, they have the right to turn the group around and attempt another trail.

Trail Rules

  • Trail Tours believes strongly that our forests should be protected. Riders participating in a trail tour will leave the trail looking better than they found it.
  • Riders will not deviate from the designated trail. It damages the environment.
  • Riders will keep to the right of roads and wide trails. Just like any other road, there will be other riders or oncoming traffic.
  • If travelling in a group and passing other riders on the trail, each rider will notify everyone they pass of how many are coming behind them.
